Portable 5v supply and charger

I have a bunch of Lipo and Li-ion batteries that I could only use for powering hobby stuff, and I wanted to give them a more practical use. So I made this board where you can plug a battery in and power any USB device that uses 5v and doesn't draw that much current.

Initial design

Here were my design criteria for this board: I wanted it to take 2-6s voltage, which is around 7.4-25.2V, regulate it down to 5V and power a USB port, and have a way to represent voltage so the user knows how much power is left in their battery.

For regulating the voltage, I used a LM7805 linear voltage regulator which can take 7-35V in and output 5V 1.5A. For powering the USB port, I used a TPS2513 chip, which connects to the D+ and D- of a USB port and matches the voltage it sees. For example, if you had an iphone that had 2.7V on D+ and 2.0V on D-, the chip would bring its own data pins to the same voltage, allowing the iphone to accept the USB connection. For representing the battery charge, I used a 1/5 voltage divider, a Seeed Xiao SAMD21 microcontroller, and 4 LEDs. The microcontroller detects the voltage of the battery, calculates how many cells the battery has, calculates the average voltage per cell, then turns on a certain amount of LEDs based on the cell voltage. For example, a fully charged Lipo has 4.2V per cell, so 4 LEDs would turn on.

I used KiCAD to design the board, JLCPCB for fabrication, and I bought all the parts from Digikey and hand soldered everything (BAD IDEA)
