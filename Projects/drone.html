Long range fpv drone

The goal of this project was to build a long range fpv drone for filming videos and such. I also wanted to make the drone as un-losable as possible. I included GPS return to home and position hold, so if I lost signal the drone could try to fly back by itself. I also added GPS telemetry logs and beepers, so if the drone crashed, I would have the approximate coordinates and the beeper would make noise and help me find it.

Parts

Frame: Phisital Aries4 4 inch foldable frame

I chose to go with a 4-inch sized frame because they have a good balance of efficiency and weight. While 5-inch drones are usually pretty heavy (like 750g+), 4-inch drones are usually just stretched out 3-inch drones, and it's reasonable for them to weigh around 250g, and the extra propeller diameter is more efficient.

Flight controller: Speedybee F405 Mini

ESC: JHEMCU EM40 40A 2-6s 4in1 ESC

This ESC only cost $17 on aliexpress! Very cheap and this drone isn't going to draw crazy current anyway.

Motors and props: HappyModel 1404 4800KV, Gemfan 4024 2 blade

Reciever: Radiomaster RP4TD

This reciever has true diversity (two recievers and two antennas that automatically switch). Radio link will rarely be a problem on this drone, the video will drop out way before the radio.

Video transmitter: HGLRC Zeus 1.6W, A powerful 1.6W analog video transmitter to get the most range as possible.

GPS: HGLRC M100 mini, A decent GPS, gets a lock in 5 minutes from a cold start.

Beeper: Vifly Finder 2

This beeper has its own battery and can beep for a day, even if the main drone battery is dead. It can be configured to beep on power loss, radio loss, or a manual switch.

Analog camera: Caddx ANT lite

Action camera: Runcam Thumb 2, A budget camera (~$100) that can film up to 4K60FPS

Results

Without the battery and including the action camera, the drone ended up weighing 190g, which is a bit on the heavy side but oh well.

Flight time and range

With the action camera and using a 4S 850 mAh battery, I was able to get around 10 minutes of flight time just hovering in position hold mode. While flying around semi-agressively, the drone can cover about 8-10km of total distance.

Autonomous performance

I used Betaflight's GPS return to home and position hold features, and the drone reliably returns home. Betaflight's autoland feature still leaves much to be desired, so usually I just manually land it. However, I didn't use a magnetometer (compass) so the drone has to calculate heading through GPS and inertial measurement, which isn't ideal for autonomous flight. The position hold is also just alright, it drifts a few feet, and doesn't really hover perfectly still. This is expected, because the drone only has GPS, barometer, and IMU, as GPS and barometer are only accurate up to a few feet. Usually commerical drones have way better position hold because they have extra sensors like lidar and optical flow sensors, which are more precise at close distances. I wouldn't leave it alone in position hold mode.

Extra antenna stuff

This project really got me thinking about basic RF concepts like antenna gain and transmission power. So I decided to try building my own high gain helical antenna. I used a metal lid as a ground plane, and used a 3d-printed jig to coil wire around to make a 7 turn helical antenna tuned for 5.8 gHz. Then I used a coaxial cable with an SMA connector and soldered the ground to the ground plane, and the signal wire to the antenna's active element. I estimated the antenna to have a gain of about 13.5 dBi, and a half power beamwidth of 40 degrees using an online calculator. I was able to get about 2.5km away on 800 mW using it, and then I got scared and turned around.
